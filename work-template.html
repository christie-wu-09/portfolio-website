Design Question

The word NFT often bears the connotations of scams, fads, and money laundry schemes as it embeds financial, technological, and social implications. How can design engage people to look at the intangible world of NFT differently and critically?

Design Question

Overview

We’re All Gonna Make It (WAGMI) To The Moon is a collection of board games that navigate directly into the confusing but fascinating world of Non-Fungible Token (NFT) art. Owing to the explosion of the NFT market in 2021, user-generated art, often artless and even kitsch, has sometimes skyrocketed to be as valuable as the artworks in galleries and museums. The games let the players see the appeal and issues of the NFT technology, experience what it means to be an NFT artist/investor, and wrap our heads around “WTF is NFT art?”

First game: WAGMI

The first game WAGMI allows players to play the role of NFT artists, so they can learn about the nuances that constitute NFT art projects and the niche NFT community while indulging in rapid art-making.

*WAGMI is a crypto slang meaning “we are all gonna make it” in the NFT community

Second game: To The Moon

The other game To The Moon let the players become NFT investors. The game simulates the random chance of success, collective experiences of value, and cultural and social implications of the NFT art market.

*To the moon is a crypto slang meaning when the price of the coin or the art piece reaches a skyrocketing price

Design Process

  1. Game Design Research
  2. Topic Research
  3. Prototyping
  4. Design and Iterations
  5. Usability Testing

Game Design Research: Research games that explore the question of value and art-making.
Topic Research:
- Identify issues and implications of NFT technology.
- Interview experts and artists to understand their experiences with NFT.
- Consult a lawyer around issues of NFT.
- Analyze and conduct visual studies on NFT art and the NFT art platform.
